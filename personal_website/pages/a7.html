Let's talk about MusicLM, an AI model created by Google that can generate high-fidelity music from text descriptions suchs as "calm ambience forest music with a violin and a guitar playing the melody along with a piano in the background".
The model takes in the text prompt and can cast the process of conditional music generation pieces as a sequence-to-sequence modeling task, generating music at 24 kHz that remains consistent over several minutes.
Another experiments claim that MusicLM can outperforms previous systems both in audio quality and adherence to the text description it takes as input. Additionally, Google research department has demonstrated that the tool can be conditioned on both text and a melody in that it can transform whistled and hummed melodies according to the style described in a text prompt. I know, this sounds CRAZY, and also they have publicly release some samples and their dataset for this model.
